
# Natural Language Processing (NLP)

## What is NLP?

**Natural Language Processing (NLP)** is a field of **Artificial Intelligence (AI)** that helps computers **understand, interpret, and generate human language**.

It enables **machines to communicate with humans** using natural language such as English.

---

# Key Challenges in NLP

## 1. Ambiguity

Ambiguity occurs when **words or sentences have multiple possible meanings**, making it difficult for machines to understand the correct interpretation.

### a. Pragmatic Ambiguity

The meaning of a sentence depends on the **speaker’s intention or the situation**, not just the words.

**Example:**
*"Can you open the window?"*
Literally asks about ability, but actually **means a request to open the window**.

---

### b. Lexical Ambiguity

Occurs when a **single word has more than one meaning**.

**Example:**
*"She went to the bank."*
The word **bank** could mean a **financial institution** or a **river bank**.

---

### c. Syntactic Ambiguity

Occurs when the **structure of a sentence allows multiple interpretations**.

**Example:**
*"I saw the man with a telescope."*
This could mean **I used a telescope to see the man**, or **the man had the telescope**.

---

### d. Anaphoric Ambiguity

Occurs when a **pronoun refers to more than one possible noun in a sentence**.

**Example:**
*"Ravi told Arjun that he won."*
The pronoun **he** could refer to **Ravi or Arjun**.

---

## 2. Lack of Standardization

Language varies across **regions, cultures, and writing styles**, which makes processing difficult.

**Example:**
USA, U.S.A, and US all represent the **same country but are written differently**.

---

## 3. Ethical Considerations

NLP systems must address issues such as:

* **Bias in AI models**
* **User privacy**
* **Responsible use of AI**

It is important to ensure that **user data is safe and models are fair**.

---

## 4. Context Understanding

Understanding the **context of a sentence** is challenging because the meaning of words can change depending on the situation.

Example:
A sentence may be interpreted differently if the **context is missing or misunderstood**.

---

## 5. Data Scarcity

Some domains or languages have **limited training data**, making it harder to build accurate NLP models.

This is especially common in **specialized fields or low-resource languages**.

---

# Linguistic Levels Used in NLP

NLP is closely related to **lingistics**, which studies human language.

### 1. Phonetics

Study of **speech sounds** in a language.

### 2. Morphology

Study of the **structure of words and how they are formed**.

### 3. Syntax

Study of **sentence structure and grammar rules**.

### 4. Semantics

Study of the **meaning of words and sentences**.
